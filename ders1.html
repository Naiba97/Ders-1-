Ontario ɒnˈtɛərioʊ on-TAIR-ee-oh French: [ɔ̃taʁjo]) is one of the thirteen provinces and territories of Canada. [9][note 1] Located in Central Canada [10] Ontario is the country's most populous province. As of the 2021 Canadian census, it is home to 38.5 percent of the country's population, and is the second-largest province by total area (after Quebec). Ontario is Canada's fourth-largest jurisdiction in total area of all the Canadian provinces and territories.[11] It is home to the nation's capital, Ottawa and its most populous city, Toronto [12] which is Ontario's provincial capital.

Etimology

Ontario is a term thought to be derived from Indigenous origins, either Ontarí:io, a Huron (Wyandot) word meaning "great lake", [15] or possibly skanadario, which means "beautiful water" or "sparkling water"in the

Iroquoian languages.
